Scaling of VaR: Assumptions

It is often the practice to convert the 1-period value at risk (VaR) into an equivalent VaR estimate for a period with a different length T, by scaling the 1-period VaR with the square root of T.

We shall discuss here the assumptions underlying the validity of the above transformation. This shall allow us to also identify the conditions under which the scaling of the 1-period VaR may cease to remain valid.
